## IMPPA asks PM Modi's help in lifting 'Bengal Files' ban
### Synopsis
The Indian Motion Picture Producers' Association (IMPPA) has written to Prime Minister Narendra Modi over the ongoing controversy surrounding The Bengal Files. The film was released on September 5, 2025 but is reportedly facing an unofficial ban in West Bengal, with most theaters refusing to screen it. Despite being certified by the Central Board of Film Certification (CBFC) and legally permitted for public exhibition, the film is not being screened due to a collective decision by theater owners.

## Producers, distributors facing intimidation: IMPPA
### Film's plight
IMPPA's letter was addressed to Prime Minister Narendra Modi, said, "Though not officially banned, the film has been subjected to indirect restrictions that have denied the public its rightful opportunity to view it." The association said producers and distributors are facing intimidation and obstacles, without claiming any government subsidies, and did not single out central authorities as the source of intimidation. They also alleged that the state government had failed to provide a safe environment for the film's release.

## FWICE also raised its voice against the ban
### Ongoing debates
The controversy surrounding The Bengal Files has sparked ongoing debates about censorship, politics, and artistic rights, the film has been described as a serious, controversial work dealing with sensitive historical events. The film delves into sensitive topics such as the 1946 Calcutta riots, which adds to its controversial nature. Earlier, the Federation of Western India Cine Employees (FWICE) had condemned the ban, calling it "shocking" that a CBFC-certified film faced such restrictions.

## Film disrespects Gopal Patha, claims grandson
### Family's disappointment
The film has also come under fire for its portrayal of Gopal Patha (also referred to as Gopal Mukherjee in some references), a key figure purportedly protecting Hindus during the 1946 riots—not a British police officer. His grandson expressed disappointment over the portrayal and filed a complaint against director Vivek Agnihotri. A complaint was filed by the grandson; the family did not deny legal action in that regard.
